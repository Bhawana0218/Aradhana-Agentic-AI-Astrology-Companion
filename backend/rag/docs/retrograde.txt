Retrograde motion in astrology occurs when a planet appears to move backward in the sky from our perspective on Earth. This optical illusion happens because of the relative orbital speeds of Earth and the other planets. While all planets go retrograde at different intervals, the meaning is universally about turning inward, reviewing, and redoing.

Mercury retrograde is the most well-known and occurs 3-4 times per year for about 3 weeks at a time. During Mercury retrograde, the planet of communication, travel, and technology appears to reverse course. Common themes include miscommunications, travel delays, technology glitches, and encounters with people from the past. Rather than fearing Mercury retrograde, it is best understood as a time to slow down, double-check details, and review unfinished business. It is excellent for editing, research, and reconnecting with old friends.

Venus retrograde occurs about every 18 months and lasts approximately 6 weeks. During Venus retrograde, issues of love, values, self-worth, and relationships come up for review. Past relationships may resurface, and you may question what you truly value. Venus retrograde is not ideal for starting new relationships but is excellent for healing old relationship wounds and reassessing your values.

Mars retrograde occurs about every 26 months and lasts approximately 2-2.5 months. Mars retrograde is a time when your drive, ambition, and assertiveness turn inward. You may feel frustrated or blocked in taking action, but this is a time to reconsider your goals and strategies rather than push forward. Anger management and understanding your desires are key themes.

Jupiter retrograde occurs annually for about 4 months. During Jupiter retrograde, growth and expansion turn inward. It is a time to review your beliefs, philosophies, and higher education plans. The external luck of Jupiter turns into internal wisdom during this period.

Saturn retrograde occurs annually for about 4.5 months. Saturn retrograde is a time for reviewing your responsibilities, boundaries, and long-term goals. You may feel the weight of your obligations more heavily, but this is an opportunity to restructure your approach to discipline and achievement.

Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto retrograde for about 5 months each year. Because these planets move so slowly, their retrograde periods are less personally noticeable but still significant for collective themes. Uranus retrograde turns innovation inward, Neptune retrograde increases spiritual discernment, and Pluto retrograde deepens inner transformation.

A planet that is retrograde in the birth chart indicates an area where your energy turns inward. A natal retrograde planet often indicates a need to develop this planet's energy in a non-traditional way. For example, Mercury retrograde in the natal chart often indicates a unique, deep way of thinking and communicating that may not be immediately understood by others.
