The Moon's phases are powerful astrological markers that reflect the cycle of growth, manifestation, release, and renewal. Each phase carries a specific energy that can guide timing for intentions, actions, and reflection.

The New Moon occurs when the Sun and Moon are conjunct in the same sign. This is a time of new beginnings, setting intentions, and planting seeds for the lunar cycle ahead. The energy is fresh, introspective, and full of potential. In the birth chart, the New Moon phase indicates a person who is instinctual, subjective, and emotionally responsive. New Moons are excellent for starting new projects, beginning new habits, and setting intentions for what you want to grow.

The Waxing Crescent occurs about 3-4 days after the New Moon, when a sliver of the Moon is visible. This is a time of building momentum, taking initiative, and pushing forward with the intentions set at the New Moon. It requires faith and action.

The First Quarter Moon occurs about 7 days after the New Moon, when the Moon is half-lit and at a square to the Sun. This brings challenges, decisions, and the need to overcome obstacles. First Quarter energy is dynamic and action-oriented, demanding that you make choices to stay aligned with your intentions.

The Waxing Gibbous occurs about 10-11 days after the New Moon. This is a time of refinement, analysis, and preparation. The energy supports fine-tuning your approach, gathering resources, and perfecting your plan before the culmination at the Full Moon.

The Full Moon occurs when the Sun and Moon are in opposite signs, about 14-15 days after the New Moon. This is a time of culmination, harvest, heightened emotions, and illumination. Full Moons bring things to light, reveal what has been hidden, and mark the peak of the lunar cycle. In the birth chart, a Full Moon phase indicates a person who is objective, responsive, and relationship-oriented.

The Waning Gibbous occurs about 17-18 days after the New Moon. This is a time of gratitude, sharing, and teaching. The energy supports giving back, disseminating what you have learned, and celebrating your harvest before releasing.

The Last Quarter Moon occurs about 21-22 days after the New Moon, when the Moon is again half-lit but waning. This is a time of release, forgiveness, and letting go. Last Quarter energy supports evaluation, surrender, and clearing out what no longer serves you.

The Waning Crescent (Balsamic Moon) occurs in the final days before the next New Moon. This is a time of rest, dreaming, surrender, and spiritual renewal. The energy is deeply introspective and intuitive, supporting meditation, rest, and preparation for the next cycle.

Lunar phases in the birth chart reveal your basic emotional nature and how you process life. Those born during the New Moon phase are often pioneering and self-motivated. Those born during the Full Moon are highly relational and attuned to others. Eclipse seasons (Solar and Lunar eclipses) are intensified New and Full Moons that bring fated events and accelerated growth.
