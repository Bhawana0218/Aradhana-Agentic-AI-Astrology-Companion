Chiron is an asteroid-comet hybrid discovered in 1977, named after the wise centaur healer of Greek mythology. In astrology, Chiron represents the wounded healer archetype: our deepest wound, the area where we feel most insecure and inadequate, and paradoxically, where we have the greatest potential to heal others.

Chiron's placement by sign and house in the birth chart reveals the nature of your core wound and the path to healing it. The Chiron wound is not a surface-level issue but a deep, often existential pain that we carry from early life or from past incarnations. It is the area where we feel broken, unworthy, or fundamentally flawed.

Chiron in Aries or the 1st House wounds relate to identity and self-assertion. These individuals may struggle with feeling unseen, inadequate, or unable to assert themselves. Healing comes through courageously claiming their identity and showing others how to do the same.

Chiron in Taurus or the 2nd House wounds relate to self-worth and material security. These individuals may struggle with feelings of unworthiness, financial insecurity, or a disconnection from their body and sensuality. Healing comes through building authentic self-worth and helping others recognize theirs.

Chiron in Gemini or the 3rd House wounds relate to communication and intellectual validation. These individuals may feel misunderstood, unheard, or intellectually inadequate. Healing comes through finding their authentic voice and facilitating communication for others.

Chiron in Cancer or the 4th House wounds relate to emotional nurturing and family. These individuals may have deep wounds around maternal care, belonging, or emotional vulnerability. Healing comes through creating safe emotional spaces for themselves and others.

Chiron in Leo or the 5th House wounds relate to creative expression and recognition. These individuals may feel their creative gifts are not worthy or that they cannot shine authentically. Healing comes through expressing their unique creativity and empowering others to do the same.

Chiron in Virgo or the 6th House wounds relate to perfection, service, and health. These individuals may struggle with an inner critic, health challenges, or feeling they are never good enough. Healing comes through accepting imperfection and redefining service as sacred.

Chiron in Libra or the 7th House wounds relate to relationships and fairness. These individuals may experience repeated patterns of unfair relationships, codependency, or difficulty with balance. Healing comes through establishing healthy relationship boundaries and modeling balanced partnerships.

Chiron in Scorpio or the 8th House wounds relate to intimacy, trust, and transformation. These individuals may have deep wounds around betrayal, loss, or sexuality. Healing comes through embracing vulnerability and guiding others through their darkest transformations.

Chiron in Sagittarius or the 9th House wounds relate to faith, meaning, and truth. These individuals may struggle with spiritual crisis, disillusionment with belief systems, or a sense of meaninglessness. Healing comes through discovering authentic faith and helping others find meaning.

Chiron in Capricorn or the 10th House wounds relate to authority, achievement, and public standing. These individuals may feel burdened by responsibility, inadequate in their career, or wounded by authority figures. Healing comes through redefining success and becoming a compassionate authority.

Chiron in Aquarius or the 11th House wounds relate to belonging and individuality. These individuals may feel like outcasts, alienated from groups, or unable to reconcile their uniqueness with community. Healing comes through embracing their uniqueness and creating inclusive communities.

Chiron in Pisces or the 12th House wounds relate to spiritual connection, boundaries, and transcendence. These individuals may experience wounds around escapism, victimhood, or spiritual confusion. Healing comes through developing spiritual discernment and channeling compassion without losing themselves.
