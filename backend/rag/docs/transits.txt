Transits are the current positions of planets as they move through the sky and form aspects to your natal birth chart. They are one of the most important tools for timing in astrology, showing what energies are being activated in your life at any given time.

The outer planets (Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) make the most significant transits because they move slowly and their effects last months or years. Saturn transits occur every 29.5 years and mark major milestones of maturation and responsibility. The Saturn return around ages 29-30, 58-60, and 87-89 is a pivotal life passage where you face your karmic lessons.

Uranus transits last about 7 years per sign and bring sudden changes, breakthroughs, and liberation. The Uranus opposition around age 42 and the Uranus return around age 84 are periods of major life restructuring. Uranus transits to personal planets often precede unexpected events that ultimately free you from limiting situations.

Neptune transits last about 14 years per sign and dissolve boundaries, heighten intuition, and can bring confusion or spiritual awakening. Neptune transiting the angles or personal planets often coincides with periods of creative inspiration, spiritual seeking, or experiences of disillusionment that lead to deeper truth.

Pluto transits are the most transformative, lasting anywhere from 12 to 30 years depending on the sign. Pluto transits to personal planets signal profound death-rebirth cycles that transform your identity at the deepest level. Pluto transiting the 1st house, for example, often coincides with a complete reinvention of self.

Jupiter transits last about one year per sign and bring expansion, opportunity, and growth. Jupiter's transit through your 1st house is often a year of personal growth and confidence. Jupiter transits to your Sun or Moon are generally favorable periods.

The faster planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars) make shorter but more frequent transits. The Moon transits each sign in 2.5 days, triggering emotional responses. Mercury retrograde periods (3-4 times per year) are famous for communication mix-ups and technology glitches but are also excellent for review and reflection.

Orbs for transits are typically 1-2 degrees for outer planets and 3-5 degrees for personal planets. The exact transit is usually the most powerful, but the effects can be felt within the orb range. Transits are usually most noticeable when they activate a natal planet by hard aspect (conjunction, opposition, square) or when they cross the angles (Ascendant, IC, Descendant, MC).

When interpreting transits, consider the condition of the transiting planet by sign and house, the condition of the natal planet being activated, and the nature of the aspect being formed. A challenging transit to a well-placed natal planet is easier to manage than a harmonious transit to a poorly placed one.
