Astrology offers deep insight into career paths, wealth potential, and professional fulfillment through the houses that govern work, money, and public life. The key houses for career and wealth are the 2nd, 6th, and 10th houses, along with the signs and planets that occupy or rule them.

The 2nd House governs earned income, personal resources, values, and self-worth. Planets in the 2nd house show how you make money and what you value in material terms. Taurus on the cusp or Venus strong in the chart often indicates steady earning potential. Jupiter in the 2nd house can indicate financial expansion, while Saturn here suggests financial discipline learned through early limitations. The sign on the 2nd house cusp colors your approach to money. Aries on the 2nd means bold earning approaches, while Virgo suggests careful budgeting and service-based income.

The 6th House governs daily work, employment, skills, coworkers, and service. This house shows the nature of your work environment, your relationship with colleagues, and the skills you develop through work. Mercury-ruled Virgo on the 6th house cusp suggests analytical or communication-based work. Mars here can indicate a competitive work environment or work involving physical energy. The 6th house also reveals your health practices and how you maintain wellness through daily routines.

The 10th House is the Midheaven (MC) and rules career, reputation, public standing, and life direction. This is the most important house for career astrology. The sign on the MC cusp and any planets in the 10th house reveal your professional path and how you are seen by the world. Capricorn or Saturn strong on the MC suggests a career in business, government, or structured fields. Leo on the MC indicates creative leadership or performance. Aries on the MC suggests pioneering or entrepreneurial work.

The 10th house ruler and its placement by sign and house adds crucial detail. For example, if the ruler of the 10th is in the 1st house, your career identity is deeply personal and visible. If in the 4th house, home-based business or real estate may be significant. The condition of Saturn is also critical for career astrology, as Saturn naturaly rules the 10th house.

The 8th House governs shared resources, investments, inheritances, and financial partnerships. Pluto or Scorpio influence here can indicate transformative financial experiences, including inheritances or significant investments. The 8th house also rules taxes, debts, and financial intimacy in partnerships.

Planetary aspects to career points add nuance. A strong Sun-MC aspect suggests visibility and leadership in career. Saturn-MC aspects bring responsibility and slow but steady achievement. Jupiter-MC aspects bring opportunities and growth through career. Challenging aspects to the MC can indicate obstacles or public controversies that ultimately strengthen your path.

When analyzing career potential, astrologers also look at the North Node placement, which shows the direction of professional growth, and the Part of Fortune, which reveals where natural talent and financial ease converge.
