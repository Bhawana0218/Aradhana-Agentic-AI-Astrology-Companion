In astrology, the houses represent the different areas of life where planetary energies manifest. While signs show how energy expresses, houses show where it expresses in your life. The house system divides the birth chart into 12 segments, each governing specific domains.

The First House represents the self, the body, appearance, and how you present yourself to the world. It is associated with Aries and Mars. Planets in the first house strongly color your personality and approach to life. The cusp of the first house is your Ascendant or Rising Sign.

The Second House governs money, possessions, values, self-worth, and material resources. It is associated with Taurus and Venus. This house shows how you earn and spend money, what you value, and your relationship with material security.

The Third House rules communication, siblings, local travel, early education, and the immediate environment. It is associated with Gemini and Mercury. This house reveals how you think, speak, learn, and connect with neighbors and siblings.

The Fourth House represents home, family, roots, ancestry, and your private life. It is associated with Cancer and the Moon. The cusp of the fourth house is the Imum Coeli (IC). This house shows your foundation, your relationship with your parents, and where you find emotional security.

The Fifth House governs creativity, romance, children, pleasure, and self-expression. It is associated with Leo and the Sun. This house reveals how you express your creative gifts, approach romantic relationships, and experience joy and play.

The Sixth House rules health, daily work, service, routines, and pets. It is associated with Virgo and Mercury. This house shows your approach to health and wellness, your daily habits, and your attitude toward service and work.

The Seventh House governs partnerships, marriage, relationships, and open enemies. It is associated with Libra and Venus. The cusp of the seventh house is the Descendant. This house reveals what you seek in committed relationships and how you relate one-on-one.

The Eighth House represents transformation, shared resources, intimacy, death and rebirth, and the occult. It is associated with Scorpio and Pluto. This house governs inheritances, taxes, sexual intimacy, and deep psychological transformation.

The Ninth House rules higher education, philosophy, religion, travel, and the search for meaning. It is associated with Sagittarius and Jupiter. This house reveals your beliefs, your quest for wisdom, and your experiences with foreign cultures and spiritual exploration.

The Tenth House governs career, reputation, public standing, and life direction. It is associated with Capricorn and Saturn. The cusp of the tenth house is the Midheaven or MC. This house shows your professional path, your ambitions, and how you are seen by the world.

The Eleventh House rules friendships, social networks, groups, hopes, and wishes. It is associated with Aquarius and Uranus. This house reveals your circle of friends, your involvement in communities, and your hopes and aspirations for the future.

The Twelfth House represents the subconscious, dreams, solitude, hidden enemies, and spiritual transcendence. It is associated with Pisces and Neptune. This house governs what is hidden, your deepest fears, karma, and the space of spiritual retreat and healing.

The house system used most commonly is Placidus, which is based on time and produces houses of varying sizes. Equal house and Whole Sign systems are also popular. The angular houses (1st, 4th, 7th, 10th) are considered most powerful, followed by succedent houses (2nd, 5th, 8th, 11th), and cadent houses (3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th).
